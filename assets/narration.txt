AIonOS opens with Safran's production floor and shows how existing CCTV feeds can be turned into one live operational cockpit.

The vision layer watches every zone continuously, converting camera footage into counts, movement patterns, congestion signals, and station-level status.

From there, supervisors see operations control in one place: active lines, work-in-progress, hand-off delays, dwell time, and emerging bottlenecks.

The same agents measure productivity by tracking cycle adherence, idle windows, queue build-up, staffing imbalance, and asset utilisation across the floor.

Safety and compliance are monitored at the same time, with PPE checks, restricted-area breaches, unsafe motion, and escalation triggers backed by visual evidence.

Instead of just showing footage, the cockpit recommends action: rebalance work, intervene at a delayed station, inspect an exception, or validate a compliance breach.

Managers can move from camera view to decision view, using AI reasoning to understand why throughput is slipping and what to change next.

Taken together, Safran Vision Agents turn CCTV into a production intelligence layer for operations control, productivity improvement, safety, and compliance.
